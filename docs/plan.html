Evolution: your week, synthesized, and a podcast you can argue with

Every input you consume and output you produce flows into one place. Each week it writes you a review of what you learned, then turns that review into audio you can listen to on a walk and interrupt to ask questions out loud, in English or 中文. Not a demo requirement, but the thing you will actually use. Cloud-first on Modal.

1. The vision

The idea in one line Instead of you doing the weekly reflection by hand, the system gathers everything you read, watched, searched, coded, and learned, and hands you back a synthesis you can read in Notion, hear as a podcast, and question by voice while you walk. Your Entertainment DB and your existing Notion weekly journal are part of the raw material, not replaced by it.

Closest existing product is Exist.io (it aggregates GitHub, read-later, and media into weekly correlations), but it has no Notion output, no AI-coding-session capture, and no audio. Nobody turns a personal learning log into a podcast you can interrogate. That is the white space, and it is very you.

4. The weekly review (approach A + C)

A · The weekly mirror

Every Sunday a Modal cron reads the week from every source and writes a Notion "Weekly Review" page plus a phone digest: what you learned, the pattern that emerged, the mistake that became a lesson, and what you keep relearning. Capped at 2 to 6 themes plus one recurring-lesson callback, so it is signal, not noise (the same "3+ occurrences = a pattern" rule your vault already uses, which news-digest engines also use).

C · The proactive coach

During the week, not just Sundays, it surfaces the right past lesson before you need it: "you are starting a phase-gate review; the last 3 skips caused regressions, want the checklist?"

Your week 🧠
14 sessions · 5 corrections · 1 new pattern
📥 9 AI-agent videos · 6 articles · 1 weekly-journal entry

Promoted to a pattern: "re-ground in the plan before asserting" (3rd time)
You keep relearning: verify the deploy profile (4th)
→ Read in Notion · 🎧 Listen
⚡ Just-in-time: new concept this week: "x402 payments." Want a 3-min explainer you can question on your walk?

5. Listen, then ask out loud (the part you've wanted)

The honest research result: NotebookLM's "Join and ask" is real and verbal, but English-only, manual, and not automatable. So we use a two-layer design from one synthesis.

Layer 1 · Listen

🎧 NotebookLM podcast

Your weekly synthesis becomes a NotebookLM Audio Overview (Deep Dive, focused on "this week"), generated automatically through the NotebookLM MCP you already have: create notebook → add the synthesis → make audio → download the mp3 to your phone. Supports Chinese. This is the "press play on a walk" layer.

Layer 2 · Ask

🎙️ OpenAI Realtime voice agent

For interrupting and asking out loud, a small Realtime voice agent grounded on the same synthesis (you have the key). Real-time, barge-in, bilingual EN + 中文, fully private, runs on your phone over WebRTC. About $0.05 to $0.10 a minute with caching. This is the "wait, explain x402 again" layer that NotebookLM cannot automate.

Why this matches your goal exactly You wanted: hear a review, and whenever a new concept shows up, ask about it verbally to understand it deeply, hands-free on a walk. Layer 1 gives you the produced episode; Layer 2 gives you the conversation. If you want one tool, the Realtime agent alone can both narrate and answer, NotebookLM just makes a nicer episode.

8. Honest notes

TopicThe real consideration
Vault to cloudRead the vault from a private git repo (you commit hourly), not from your B2 restic backup (that is encrypted and meant for recovery, keep it as is).
NotebookLM limitsIts interactive "Join" is English-only, manual, and has no API. The audio-generation MCP is unofficial (could break if Google changes internals). So we treat the podcast as the nice-to-have and the Realtime agent as the dependable ask-layer.
Insight needs timeCross-source correlations ("you read about agents, then shipped agent code") need a few weeks of data before they are meaningful. Early weeks = simple, honest summaries.
PrivacyIt is your most personal data. Everything stays in your Notion + your cloud, nothing sold, your keys.
