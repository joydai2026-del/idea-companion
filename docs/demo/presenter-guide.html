1. The story in 30 seconds

The one sentence to open with "Notion is the database, the dashboard, and the reading surface for a personal AI product. The voice agent is just how I capture ideas when I am walking and cannot type."

The problem. Your best thinking happens on a walk, away from a keyboard. By the time you sit down, it is gone.

The product. Speak to a tutor hands-free. Every walk becomes a Notion Conversation. Anything you ask for out loud becomes a Notion Report: a real teaching workspace with a summary, concept cards, a glossary, a practice loop, a quiz, cited sources, and an illustration. Then Telegram pings you the link.

Why it is a Notion demo, not a voice demo The clever part is not that it talks. It is that the learning system lives inside Notion: the report is built by a Notion Worker running in Notion's own runtime, and the finished lesson is a Notion page you keep, search, and revisit. Voice is the on-ramp; Notion is the home.

5. The Notion Worker migration (your headline talking point)

This is the part that makes a Notion audience lean in. The report builder used to be a plain Modal job. It now runs inside Notion.

Before
  • Modal captured the voice and built the report.
  • Notion was just a place rows landed.
  • The "AI product" lived entirely outside Notion.
After
  • Modal only captures the voice (it needs browser audio).
  • A Notion Worker builds the lesson inside Notion's runtime.
  • Notion is now the operating surface, not the afterthought.
The line to say "The live capture needs a microphone, so it stays on Modal. But the learning system itself runs inside Notion. The Report row triggers a Notion Worker, and the Worker turns it into a page that teaches me, quizzes me, and cites its sources."

Worth knowing if asked: a fully Notion-native "Custom Agent" cannot call an external image API, so a Worker (which can) is the right Notion primitive for this job. That is exactly the Custom Agent vs Worker Agent distinction, and it is a clean thing to explain live.

7. Pre-demo checklist (run 10 minutes before)

  • Health endpoint returns ok with has_notion_worker: true.
  • Health shows Notion DBs present and auth enforced.
  • Telegram opens the Mini App on your phone.
  • Phone is on Wi-Fi, microphone permission already granted.
  • Notion dashboard open in a clean browser tab, Reports grouped by Status.
  • Do one full dry run: talk, request a report, confirm the Ready + link ping arrives.
  • Have one finished report pre-made as a backup to show instantly.
  • Screen mirroring tested (phone + laptop on the projector).
  • Notifications silenced except the demo bot.
  • Know your demo topic and your opening line cold.
The one check that matters most The full dry run, end to end, on the same build you will demo. If the "Ready + link" Telegram message lands on your phone, the whole loop is proven. Reports building and Status flipping to Ready is the real success signal.

8. If live voice misbehaves (graceful fallback)

Voice is phone and network dependent. If the room Wi-Fi fights you, pivot without apology. The Notion loop is the point, not the microphone.

9. Questions you may get, and crisp answers

If they askYou say
Why not build the whole thing inside Notion?The live microphone needs a public web page and browser audio, which a Notion Worker does not host. So Modal captures; Notion builds and stores. Best tool for each job.
What is a Notion Worker, exactly?Code that runs inside Notion's own platform and responds to a webhook. Unlike a no-code Custom Agent, a Worker can call external APIs (like image generation), which this lesson needs.
Is my data private?Yes. It is the most personal data, so it stays in my own Notion workspace and my own cloud, on my keys. Owner-only access is enforced by Telegram identity. Nothing is sold.
Are the reports accurate or hallucinated?The report model searches the live web and cites its sources, which are clickable in the page. It is told never to invent figures.
What happens if the AI fails mid-build?The Worker tells you it could not finish and asks you to retry, and Modal has a built-in fallback builder. A walk never silently dies.
Does it really do Chinese?Yes, capture and the produced lesson both support 中文. Happy to show one turn live.
What is next for this?It grows into a weekly "evolution" review that synthesizes everything I consume and produce, and reads it back to me as audio I can question on a walk.
